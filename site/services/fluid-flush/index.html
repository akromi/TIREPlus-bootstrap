Brake · Transmission · Power steering · Fuel system

Vehicle fluids degrade over time. Replacing them on schedule is one of the cheapest and most effective ways to extend the life of major systems and avoid costly repairs.

Brake fluid flush — highly recommended

Brake fluid absorbs moisture from the air. Over 2-3 years, that moisture lowers the boiling point and corrodes brake components from the inside. A brake fluid flush every 2-3 years is one of the most overlooked services in auto maintenance — and one of the most worthwhile. After a flush, most drivers immediately notice firmer pedal feel.

Transmission fluid service

Transmission fluid lubricates the gears and clutches inside your transmission. Old, burnt fluid causes harsh shifts, slipping, and shortened transmission life. Most manufacturers recommend service every 60,000 to 100,000 km. A transmission rebuild costs thousands; a flush costs a fraction of that.

Power steering fluid

Power steering fluid degrades and accumulates wear particles. Symptoms include groaning when turning, especially at low speeds, or stiff steering. A power steering flush restores smooth operation and prevents pump damage.

Fuel system / fuel injector cleaning

Carbon and varnish build up in fuel injectors over time, reducing fuel economy and engine performance. A professional fuel system service removes deposits and restores spray pattern. Especially valuable for higher-mileage vehicles.

Why these services pay for themselves

  • A brake fluid flush ($129) protects $2,000+ worth of brake hardware
  • A transmission flush ($200-300) protects a $4,000+ transmission
  • A power steering flush prevents an $800+ pump replacement
