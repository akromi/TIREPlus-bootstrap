When should you replace your tires?

  • Tread depth at or below 4/32" — winter performance suffers significantly
  • Tread depth at 2/32" — legal minimum, you must replace
  • Visible damage — sidewall bulges, deep cuts, embedded objects
  • Cracks in the rubber from age (most tires are good for 6-10 years regardless of mileage)
  • Vibration that an alignment doesn't fix
  • Uneven wear — even with new tires, an alignment check is recommended

Winter tires in Ottawa

Ottawa winters are no joke. When daytime temperatures drop below 7°C, winter tires significantly outperform all-seasons in stopping distance and traction. Most Ontario insurance companies offer a discount when you install winter tires (typically 5%). Book early — the changeover rush starts in late October and runs through November.

We also offer seasonal tire storage so you don't have to keep a second set in your garage. Ask about availability when you book.

All-season vs all-weather vs winter — what's the difference?

  • All-season: Best for spring, summer, fall. Compromise performance below 7°C. Not certified for severe snow.
  • All-weather: Has the mountain/snowflake symbol. A real year-round option for milder Canadian climates, but a true winter tire still wins in deep cold and snow.
  • Winter: Designed for cold, snow, and ice. Best stopping distance and traction in winter conditions. The right choice for Ottawa.

TPMS — Tire Pressure Monitoring System

If your TPMS warning light is on, drive in for a free pressure check. Sometimes it's just cold weather dropping pressure; sometimes it's a slow leak or a failed sensor. We diagnose, replace, and reprogram TPMS sensors for most vehicles.
