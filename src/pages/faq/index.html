All-season tires generally last 60,000 to 100,000 km depending on driving habits, vehicle, and rotation discipline. Winter tires last 4 to 6 seasons of Ottawa winters. We measure tread depth at every visit — once you're under 4/32" for winter (or 3/32" for summer), it's time to plan a replacement.

All-season tires are designed for warm and mild conditions and lose grip below 7°C — not ideal for Ottawa winters. All-weather tires carry the 3-peak-mountain-snowflake symbol and meet winter tire legal standards in Quebec; they're a reasonable compromise if you don't want to swap tires twice a year. Winter tires use a specialized rubber compound that stays flexible in cold, with significantly shorter braking distance on snow and ice — the best choice for full Ottawa winters.
