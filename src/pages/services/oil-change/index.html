Why regular oil changes matter

Engine oil lubricates, cools, and cleans your engine. Over time it breaks down and accumulates contaminants. Skipping oil changes is the fastest way to shorten your engine's life. Regular changes keep your engine running smoothly, improve fuel economy, and protect your warranty.

How often should you change your oil?

Modern manufacturer recommendations vary widely:

  • Conventional oil: every 5,000 km or 6 months
  • Synthetic blend: every 8,000 km or 8 months
  • Full synthetic: every 10,000 to 16,000 km, or as your vehicle's oil life monitor indicates

Severe driving — short trips, stop-and-go traffic, extreme cold (Ottawa winters), towing — calls for more frequent changes. Check your owner's manual or ask us.

Synthetic vs conventional — which is right for you?

Modern engines, especially turbocharged or direct-injection, almost always require synthetic. Older vehicles with simpler engines may run fine on conventional. Full synthetic costs more upfront but lasts 2–3x longer between changes, so the per-kilometre cost is similar. We'll recommend what your vehicle's manufacturer specifies — that's the safest bet.
