What is wheel alignment?

Wheel alignment is the adjustment of your vehicle's suspension angles — camber, caster, and toe — so all four wheels point straight and parallel. Proper alignment makes your tires wear evenly, your steering feel right, and your fuel economy stay optimal.

Signs you need a wheel alignment

  • Your vehicle pulls to one side on a flat, straight road
  • Steering wheel is off-center when driving straight
  • Uneven or rapid tire wear (especially inner or outer edges)
  • Steering feels loose, vague, or wandering
  • You hit a pothole, curb, or large bump recently
  • You just installed new tires

Ottawa's roads — with frequent freeze-thaw cycles, potholes, and winter potholes — are hard on suspension components. Most vehicles benefit from an alignment check once per year.
